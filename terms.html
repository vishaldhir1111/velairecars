Terms & conditions.

A clear guide to the standards, checks and responsibilities that protect every Velaire reservation.

Last updated: May 2026 Applies to UK reservations

1. Booking requests

A reservation request is not confirmed until Velaire Cars has accepted the booking, checked availability, reviewed driver eligibility where required and confirmed the relevant deposit or payment status.

We may decline or cancel a request where the vehicle is unavailable, details are incomplete, eligibility checks are not satisfied or we reasonably believe the booking presents a risk to the customer, vehicle or business.

2. Driver eligibility

The driver must hold the correct licence, meet the age and insurance criteria for the selected vehicle and provide accurate identity, contact and address details. Some vehicles may require additional checks before handover.

3. Handover and return

Velaire may complete an inspection at handover and return. The customer is expected to return the vehicle on time, in the agreed condition, with the agreed fuel or charge level and with all keys, documents and accessories supplied at handover.

4. Vehicle use

Vehicles must not be used for racing, track driving, off-road use, sub-hire, unlawful activity, smoking, carrying hazardous goods or any purpose outside the agreed hire terms. Mileage, geography and driver restrictions may apply.

5. Charges and responsibility

The customer remains responsible for agreed rental charges, deposits, late return charges, excess mileage, parking charges, congestion or clean air zone charges, tolls, fines, cleaning, fuel or charging shortfalls, lost keys and damage or insurance excess where applicable.

6. Final hire agreement

These website terms are customer-facing guidance. The final hire agreement, insurance conditions and any booking-specific written confirmation will apply to the confirmed reservation.

This page is a practical policy starter for customer clarity and should be reviewed against your final rental agreement, insurer requirements and legal advice before relying on it as the full contract.
